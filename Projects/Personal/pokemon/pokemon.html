Project Summary

When I started playing Pokémon Sword in 2019, I was just learning how to make more complex computer programs. So, as I was playing the game, I started to analyse how the game was put together and how those pieces interacted. After breaking down those large problems into more manageable problems, I realized that I knew how to solve some of those smaller problems by using techniques that I was learning in my schoolwork and personal experimentation. I have been a fan of the games for a while now, desipite Pokémon Let's Go Pikachu (2018) was my first game I played in the series, and have wondered how I would go about writing a Pokémon game. That's when the idea hit me, I could try to create my own Pokémon game!

I started first by brainstorming where I would like my fan game would take place as this would determine what Pokémon I would choose to include in my Pokédex and other core mechanics. As I was ultimately decided on a region based on the lore and lands of Scandinvia. As this was my first full sized game, I decided to start on a Pokédex of 251 Pokémon, similar in size to the generation 2 games in the series.

years later and I am still working on this project. It has gone through many different iterations, and three different formats. The many different iterations that I have gone through have taught me a lot about programming and game development as a whole.

Features

Pokémon

Arguably the most important aspect of the series, I spent a long time getting the Pokémon to work and which ones I wanted to include in the region. I also wanted to include some high quality fakémon to include in the region. However, I am not a very good artist, but I was able to find some very high quality ones as part of the Create-A-Pokémon Project hosted by Smogon.

Pokédex

To help display the fantastic Pokémon found in my region and to help the player keep track of their progress catching Pokémon, I created a reimagined staple of the Pokémon series: the Pokédex. I wanted to show the Pokémon's typing in the background, so I riffed on the Poké Ball design in the background. As the user travels through the Pokédex and find different Pokémon, their primary type will dictate what background is shown. The layout was inspired by the many different fan made layouts from my google research into the design of region Pokédexs.
